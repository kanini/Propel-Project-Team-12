# User Story - US_058

## Story ID

* ID: US_058

## Story Title

* AI Safety & Operational Guardrails

## Description

* As a Compliance Officer, I want AI components to operate within defined safety and operational guardrails, so that AI outputs are controlled, fallback mechanisms exist, and the system degrades gracefully when AI services are unavailable.

## Acceptance Criteria

1. **Given** the human-in-the-loop requirement (AIR-S01), **When** AI generates any clinical suggestion, **Then** the output is flagged as "AI-Suggested" and cannot be committed to the patient record without explicit staff verification.

2. **Given** confidence thresholds (AIR-S02), **When** an AI extraction has a confidence score below the defined threshold, **Then** it is auto-flagged for mandatory manual review and highlighted with a warning indicator.

3. **Given** AI service unavailability (AIR-O03), **When** Azure OpenAI or Document Intelligence is unreachable, **Then** the system falls back to manual-only workflows within 5 seconds, displays a user-friendly notice, and queues documents for retry when services recover.

4. **Given** token and cost management (AIR-O05), **When** AI requests are made, **Then** per-request token usage and estimated cost are logged, daily aggregates are tracked, and a configurable daily ceiling triggers alerts at 80% and halts non-critical AI calls at 100%.

5. **Given** monitoring requirements (NFR-015), **When** AI operations run, **Then** model version, latency, error rates, and confidence distributions are captured in operational metrics accessible to admins.

## Edge Cases

* What happens when the AI model returns an empty or malformed response? The system discards the response, logs the incident, increments the error counter, and routes the document to manual processing.
* How does the system handle AI model version changes? Model version is captured per-extraction; historical data references the model version used for traceability.
